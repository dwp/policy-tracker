Description
Lowering the household benefit cap to £20,000 across the country and £23,000 in London for both new and existing claimants (Current £26,000)

Milestones

  • November 2016 — Provide policy advice & seek steers from ministers to inform regs and delivery, input and QA implementation/design work.
  • August 2016 — Consult SSAC
  • July 2016 — LAAs on HB regs
  • 14th June 2016 — Instruct & QA regs
  • June 2016 — Lay and make regs
  • April 2016 — Develop EM & EA

Nb. Intensive maintenance to follow

Objectives

To develop and support delivery of benefit cap policy.

Risks / Mitigations

  • Legal challenges
  • Knowledge management
  • Succession planning

Background

The Benefit Cap limits the amount of Benefit working age households can recieve, so that workless households will no longer be entitled to recieve more than £20,000 in benefit (£13,400 for single adults with no children) and £23,000 in Greater London (£15,410 for single adults with no children).
